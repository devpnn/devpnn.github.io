Hi, I'm Dev. volume_up

I'm a King Scholar at Dartmouth College, majoring in Geography and using the tools of the discipline to explore my research interests in political eco(nomy/logy), global macroeconomic governance, the social impact of transnational migration, and the theoretical relationship between the modern state, inequality, and crisis.

All these interests come together in my current project analyzing the impacts of climate change on migration to Europe. I’m drawing upon historical scholarship on European governance & trade and using empirical inputs from state-level population data & Geographic Information Systems (GIS) models. Situating all of this in a framework drawing upon critical social theory & psychoanalysis, my planned final product is a comparative study of the geographies of climate migration across Europe, and the implications this has for pan- and intra-European politics.

At Dartmouth, I have interned for the Sustainability Office, served on the advisory board of the First-Generation Student Program (FYSEP), and been an elected executive of the International Student Association. I have also advocated for divesting the Dartmouth endowment from fossil fuel corporations with Divest Dartmouth. In 2020, I was a DivestEd organizing fellow, and I trained with 20+ university-level fossil fuel divestment campaigns across the United States. I am presently organizing with the mutual aid efforts of the Dartmouth Student Union to help Dartmouth students through the economic hardships caused by the COVID-19 pandemic.

I’m currently on a gap year, tinkering with European migration datasets in Python, learning how to speak French, and working my way through an economic history reading list.
