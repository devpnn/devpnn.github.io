Hi, I'm Dev.

I'm a November 2022 graduate of Dartmouth College. I was a King Scholar and studied Geography and Geospatial Statistics. I use my qualitative and quantitative skillset to explore my research interests in political eco(nomy/logy), global macroeconomic governance, climate change & mitigation, and the linkages between the modern state, capital allocation, and the climate crisis.

I have experience working with public opinion, governmental, epidemiological, and geospatial data. My most recent statistical research was using a multi-level model to estimate the effect that living in high-disaster-risk county has on how people of differing political orientations perceive personal climate risk. Before that, I worked on research detailing statistical biases common in analyses of COVID-19 data in US prisons and modified recent econometric approaches to apply to prison epidemiology, as well as helping develop an experimental game theory approach to determine population-level strategies for collective-action problems.

Among visualization and mapping projects, I worked on series of animated data analytics communicating the public response to COVID-19, both as lifestyle choices and as emotional state. Other individual projects include an interactive, user-preference driven map of good places to eat in New Delhi, and an energy-systems map that calculates the annual carbon emissions of Chinese coal plants.

In my qualitative work, my most recent research looked at climate policy briefs and philosophical accounts of time-perception to develop a roadmap for planetary climate action based on how people perceive risks in and of the future. My earlier qualitative projects have been a comparative literature analysis of internet "memes" as (meta-)linguistic texts, as well as a preliminary social theory of power in modern India.

At Dartmouth, I have interned for the Sustainability Office, served on the advisory board of the First-Generation Student Program (FYSEP), and been an elected executive of the International Student Association. I also successfully advocated for divesting the Dartmouth endowment from fossil fuel corporations with Divest Dartmouth, and spent my second half of college organizing with the mutual aid efforts of the Dartmouth Student Union to help Dartmouth students through the economic hardships caused by the COVID-19 pandemic.
